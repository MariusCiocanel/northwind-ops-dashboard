# Leadership sync — 2026-06-04

**Present:** Dana (Ops), Marcus (Inventory), Priya (Growth)
**Purpose:** Weekly review heading into summer. Agreed on the metrics the new dashboard and morning briefing should center on.

## The picture this week

Revenue is holding but not clearly growing — we've been roughly flat for a few weeks and want to know whether that's seasonal or something slipping underneath. A few specific worries came up:

- **Midnight Roast looks like it's losing steam.** Dana's gut says the dark-roast line has been sliding several weeks running. If that's real, it's dragging the whole top line. Want this confirmed or killed with actual numbers.
- **Ethiopia single-origin keeps selling out.** Priya: it's our fastest grower right now, and Marcus keeps running it down to zero. We're leaving money on the table every time it stocks out. Need eyes on weeks-of-cover for the fast movers.
- **Returns feel high on the 2lb bags.** A handful of "stale / didn't love it" returns on the big bags. Want return rate broken out by size so we can tell if it's a real pattern or noise.
- **Subscription is the healthy part of the business.** Higher AOV, predictable. Priya wants the subscription-vs-one-time mix visible so we can tell if we're growing the good revenue or just chasing one-off Amazon orders.

## Priority metrics — put these front and center

These are what the dashboard should highlight and what the morning briefing should lead with.

**Sales**
1. **Net revenue** and **week-over-week growth** — overall, and we want to be able to see it by SKU.
2. **AOV** (average order value) — and how it differs between subscription and one-time orders.
3. **Top and bottom movers** — which SKUs are growing and which are fading (we expect Midnight near the bottom).
4. **Return rate**, broken out **by size (12oz vs 2lb)**.
5. **Subscription vs one-time mix** — share of revenue and of orders.

**Inventory**
6. **Weeks of cover** per SKU — on-hand divided by recent sales velocity.
7. **Stockout risk** — anything below its reorder point or with under ~2 weeks of cover (Ethiopia, almost certainly).
8. **Sell-through** — how fast each roast is moving relative to what we hold.
